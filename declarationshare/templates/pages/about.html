About the project

Like most ideas, Declare Share was a remix of other ideas, specifically: A mural of "I AM" statements on the wall outside of my office building and the oncoming storm of New Years’ resolutions. Although, it is not the best idea I have ever had, it is an important milestone for me personally.

I regularly come up with ideas, good and bad (fine...mostly bad) and enjoy sharing them with friends and coworkers. Some ideas are good enough to spur me to action. At the start of each new project, my excitement level is high, and I am convinced that this project will be THE ONE. Then slowly, the excitement fades, I begin to doubt everything about it, and development stops. "Just put that one with all the others on top of Almost-done Mountain..."

Recently, a friend recommended I read a few chapters of Linchpin by Seth Godin. Somewhere in the middle of the book is a chapter of pure gold titled: The Resistance. This chapter focuses on what is getting in the way you finishing your idea (spoiler: it's you) and how to overcome that resistance. Armed with this new knowledge, I set out to take an idea from start to finish. What you see on this site is the result.

The Future

Declare Share has a lot of possibilities and potential features. I decided to take the minimum viable product approach, picked a small set of features, and set a deadline. For now, it’s designed to allow anyone to publically declare and share their intentions to the world. Later? It will be so much more.
